Essential Calculus 2nd Edition - James Stewart:

Chapter 1 - Functions and Limits:

1.1 Functions and Their Representations

2 If \(f(x) = \frac{x^2-x}{x-1}\) and \(g(x) = x\) is it true that f = g?

Solution:

Simplifying f(x) to \[f(x) = \frac{x^2-x}{x-1} = \frac{x(x-1)}{x-1} = x\] We see that f(x) simplifies to x. However, looking at the denominator of f(x), we find that \(x-1 \neq 0 \Rightarrow x \neq 1\). The domain of f(x) is \(\{x|x \in \Re\), \(x \neq 1\}\) and the domain of g(x) is \(\{x|x \in \Re \}\). Since they have different domains, f can not be equal to g.
